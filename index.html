Reader

Here is my list of favorite books not in order :
1. Straight From the Gut by Jack Welch : It is a quite motivation life journey of an ex-CEO of GE(General Electric) which includes his ups and down at GE. Initially people called him Neutron Jack and world's toughest boss but later fortune called him "The Manager of the Century"
2. The Alchemist by Paulo Coehlo : I love reading his books.
3. Stay Hungry and Stay Foolish by Rashmi Bansal : I love reading inspiring stories of fellow successful entrepreneurs
4. "Aganpankh" Gujarati Translation of Wings of Fire by Dr.APJ Abdul Kalam : Inspiring life journey of "Missile Man" of India
5. The Zahir by Paulo Coehlo : I love reading his books.
6. Switch - How to Change Things When Change is Hard by Chip and Dan Health : It is a compelling, story-driven narrative research study in psychology, sociology, and other fields to shed new light on how we can effect trans-formative changebr>
7. A Whole New Mind by Daniel H. Pink : A fresh perceptive on what it takes to excel. The era of "left brain" dominance, and the Information Age that it engendered, are giving way to a new world in which "right brain" qualities-inventiveness, empathy, meaning-predominate. That's the argument at the center of this provocative and original book, which uses the two sides of our brains as a metaphor for understanding the contours of our times.
8. Nikola Tesla: Imagination and the Man That Invented the 20th Century by Sean Patrick : It is about the man who transformed the 20th century. It was a very entertaining and informative read. Full of facts that I wasn't aware of and inspiring deeds. A great book for anyone interested in science and discovery
9. Freakonomics: A Rogue Economist Explores the Hidden Side of Everything by Stevel D. Levitt & Stephen J. Dubner : My college professor Dr. A.S.Ranade recommended this. I want to read this one more time to explore the hidden side of everything.
10. The Flinch by Julien Smith : The flinch is why you don't do the work that matters, and why you won't make the hard decisions.
